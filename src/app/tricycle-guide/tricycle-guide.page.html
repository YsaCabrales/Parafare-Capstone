Tricycle Guide

Tricyle, AKA “trike”, is a three-wheeled public utility vehicle consisting of a motorcycle and an attached passenger’s side car. Tricycles have fixed routes and flexible routes where they can take you to your destination at the agreed price. These trikes are commonly found on suburban places except on major or main roads.

Step 1

Look for tricycles that are passing by. Tricycle drivers usually wear a colored vest that indicate their route.

Step 2

When hailing a tricycle just extend your arms so that the driver can see that you need a ride.

Step 3

Tell the driver where you want to be dropped off and negotiate the fare. Try and haggle the price, most tricycle drivers will agree to a lower price.

Step 4

Some drivers may not know the exact route to your destination, you can guide them throughout the way.

Step 5

When you're at your desired destination, say "PARA" to stop the vehicle. Pay the fare after getting off the trike.

Tips

If you want to pay the lowest fare, wait until the trike is filled up with passengers. If you want the tricycle exclusively, just tell the driver you want a “special” ride. He will charge a little extra but it’s more comfortable to ride alone.
